R. 9264IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVESJune 11, 2026Mr. Burlison (for himself, Mr. Ogles, and Mr. Moore of Alabama) introduced the following bill; which was referred to the Committee on the JudiciaryA BILLTo amend title 18, United States Code, to strike all exceptions to the circumstances in which it is unlawful for aliens to vote, and for other purposes. 
1.Short titleThis Act may be cited as the No Exceptions for Non-Citizens Voting Act. 2.Voting by aliens (a)In generalSection 611 of title 18, United States Code, is amended— 
(1)in subsection (a), by striking , unless— and all that follows through one or more of such Federal offices; and (2)by striking subsection (c). 
(b)Inadmissibility for unlawful votersSection 212(a)(10)(D) of the Immigration and Nationality Act (8 U.S.C. 1182(a)(10)(D)) is amended to read as follows:  (D)Unlawful votersAny alien who has voted in violation of any Federal, State, or local constitutional provision, statute, ordinance, or regulation is inadmissible.. 
(c)Deportability for unlawful votersSection 237(a)(6) of the Immigration and Nationality Act (8 U.S.C. 1227(a)(6)) is amended to read as follows:  (6)Unlawful votersAny alien who has voted in violation of any Federal, State, or local constitutional provision, statute, ordinance, or regulation is deportable..
