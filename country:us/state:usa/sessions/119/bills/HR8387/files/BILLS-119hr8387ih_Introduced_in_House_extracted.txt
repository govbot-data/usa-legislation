(2)by striking , in the absence of countervailing evidence,. (e)DefinitionsSection 101(a) of the Immigration and Nationality Act (8 U.S.C. 1101(a)) is amended by adding at the end the following: 
 
(53)The term socialism means a movement, revolutionary plan, or transitional phase between a market-driven economic model and communism or socialism where political power is sought or used, through violent or other means, by a collective class or group to— (A)restructure or advocate the restructuring of economic and social relations to reduce class distinctions; 
(B)establish or advocate government, worker, or collective control, influence, or ownership over substantially all production means or asset classes; (C)regulate or advocate the regulation of the fundamentals of market and asset supply, demand, and functions to make a supreme government or establish collective control or ownership over substantially all interests of asset owners, traders, or speculators; 
(D)suppress all threats to a single ruling political party, organized on a dictatorial basis, with so close an identity between the policies of such party and the government policies of the country in which the party exists that the government and the party constitute an indistinguishable unit; or (E)institute or advocate the economic, international, or governmental doctrines of Karl Marx or Friedrich Engels or any member, official, or revolutionary leader in a socialist party.  
(54)The term communism means a movement, revolutionary plan, or societal structure where political power is sought or used, though violent or other means, by a collective class or group to— (A)establish or advocate a classless, money-less, or stateless society; 
(B)restructure or advocate the restructuring of economic and social relations to abolish class distinctions; (C)assert or advocate state or collective control over substantially all aspects of political, cultural, social, or economic life; 
(D)enforce or advocate government, worker, or collective ownership, influence, or control over substantially all assets and markets; (E)suppress threats to a single ruling political party, organized on a dictatorial basis, with so close an identity between the policies of such party and the government policies of the country in which the party exists that the government and the party constitute an indistinguishable unit; or 
(F)institute or advocate the economic, international, or governmental doctrines of Karl Marx, Friedrich Engels, or any member, official, or revolutionary leader in a historic or current communist party. (55)The term Chinese communism means a movement, revolutionary plan, or societal structure, either beholden to the Chinese government or possessing Chinese communist characteristics where political power is sought or used, through violent or other means, by a collective class or group to— 
(A)establish or advocate a society, culture, government, or economy with Chinese communist characteristics; (B)require or advocate that companies, cultural entities, or organizations abide by Chinese communist party doctrines or dictates; 
(C)assert Chinese communist party control or influence over substantially all aspects of political, cultural, social, or economic life; (D)enforce or advocate Chinese communist party ownership, influence, or control over substantially all assets and markets; 
(E)suppress threats to a Chinese communist party, organized on a dictatorial basis, with so close an identity between the policies of such party and the government policies of the country in which the party exists that the government and the party constitute an indistinguishable unit; or (F)institute or advocate the economic, international, or governmental doctrines of Mao Zedong, Deng Xiaoping, Xi Jinping, or any member, official, or revolutionary leader in a Chinese communist party. 
(56)The term Marxism means a movement, revolutionary plan, or societal structure, either in a final or transitory stage, where political power is sought or used, through violent or other means, by a collective class or group to— (A)advocate that human history is primarily driven by class struggles between those who earn their living on asset ownership and profits and those who earn their living off their labor; 
(B)advocate that the foundation of all legal, political, cultural, societal, and social structures is class or economic standing and the destruction of such structures; (C)establish or advocate for an atheistic society or government prohibitions on private religious practices; 
(D)establish or advocate resource distribution from each person according to their ability, to each person according to their needs; or (E)institute or advocate the economic, international, or governmental doctrines of Karl Marx, Friedrich Engels, or any member, official, or revolutionary leader in a socialist, communist, or Chinese communist party. 
(57)The term Islamic fundamentalism means a movement to promote or achieve an Islamic society or enforce Islamic values where legal, religious, military, social, cultural, academic, or political power is sought or used to— (A)advocate or impose Islam as the state sanctioned religion of the United States; 
(B)advocate or enforce Islamic political, legal, military, social, cultural, or academic authority based upon or enforcing Sharia law in any setting; (C)advocate, justify, or employ militant jihad; 
(D)advocate or enact the destruction of Christian, Jewish, or other religious communities, churches, synagogues, temples, historical sites, relics, literature, property, organizations, or members; or (E)advocate or justify the destruction of western civilization, including the economic, international, or governmental doctrines of capitalism, constitutionalism, republicanism, or liberalism. 
(58)The term communist party includes— (A)the Communist Party of the United States, or any section, subsidiary, branch, affiliate, or subdivision of such party; 
(B)the Communist Political Association, or any section, subsidiary, branch, affiliate, or subdivision of such association; (C)the Communist Party of any State, foreign state, or political or geographical subdivision of such foreign state, or any section, subsidiary, branch, affiliate, or subdivision of any such party; 
(D)the direct predecessors or successors of the associations and parties described in subparagraphs (A), (B), and (C), regardless of what name such predecessor or successor may have used, may now bear, or may adopt; and (E)any communist-action or communist-front organization. 
(59)The term socialist party includes— (A)the Socialist Party of the United States, or any section, subsidiary, branch, affiliate, or subdivision of such party; 
(B)the Democratic Socialists of America, or any section, subsidiary, branch, affiliate, or subdivision of such organization; (C)the Socialist Party of any State, foreign state, or political or geographical subdivision of such foreign state, or any section, subsidiary, branch, affiliate, or subdivision of any such party; 
(D)the direct predecessors or successors of the parties described in subparagraphs (A), (B), and (C), regardless of what name such predecessor or successor may have used, may now bear, or may adopt; and (E)any socialist-action or socialist-front organization. 
(60)The term Chinese communist party includes— (A)the Communist Party of China; 
(B)the Revolutionary Committee of the Chinese Kuomintang; (C)the China Democratic League; 
(D)the China National Democratic Construction Association; (E)the China Association for Promoting Democracy; 
(F)the Chinese Peasants and Workers Democratic Party; (G)the China Zhi Gong Party; 
(H)the Jiusan Society; (I)the Taiwan Democratic Self-Government League; 
(J)the United Front Work Department; (K)any section, subsidiary, branch, affiliate, or subdivision of any party described in subparagraphs (A) through (J); 
(L)the direct predecessors or successors of any party described in subparagraphs (A) through (J), regardless of what name such group or organization may have used, may now bear, or may adopt; and (M)any Chinese communist-action or Chinese communist-front organization. 
(61)The term Islamic fundamentalist party includes— (A)the Muslim Brotherhood; 
(B)the Islamic State; (C)the Al-Nour Party; 
(D)Hamas; (E)Hezbollah; 
(F)Hizb ut Tahrir; (G)Boko Haram; 
(H)Al-Shabaab; (I)any political, legal, militant, social, cultural, or academic organization that seeks to implement Sharia law as a contractual or binding basis in any setting; 
(J)any political, legal, religious, militant, social, cultural, or academic organization that advocates, promotes, or justifies the use of militant jihad; (K)any section, subsidiary, branch, affiliate, or subdivision of any party or organization described in subparagraphs (A) through (J); 
(L)the direct predecessors or successors of any party or organization described in subparagraphs (A) through (J), regardless of what name such party or organization may have used, may now bear, or may adopt; and (M)any Islamic fundamentalist-action or Islamic fundamentalist-front organization. 
(62)The term Sharia law means any system of adjudication or contract enforcement, whether governmental or private, rationalized or proscribed, on the basis of Qur’anic, Sunnahic, ijma’ic, or qiyasic precedent, text, or reasoning. (63)The term militant jihad means the use of force, coercion, violence, or terror to— 
(A)enforce Islamic values or Sharia law; (B)enforce conversions to Islam; 
(C)achieve an Islamic political goal; or (D)establish Islam as the state religion. . 
(f)RegulationsThe Attorney General shall issue regulations as necessary to carry out the amendments made by this Act. (g)SeverabilityIf any provision of this Act, an amendment made by this Act, or the application thereof to any person or circumstance, is held invalid, the remainder of the Act or an amendment made by this Act, or the application thereof to any person or circumstance shall not be affected.
