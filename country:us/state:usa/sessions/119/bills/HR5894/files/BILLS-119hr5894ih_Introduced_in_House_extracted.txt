R. 5894IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVESOctober 31, 2025Ms. Titus introduced the following bill; which was referred to the Committee on Armed ServicesA BILLTo amend the Atomic Energy Defense Act to prohibit explosive testing of nuclear weapons conducted by the United States, and for other purposes.1.Short titleThis Act may be cited as the Renewing Efforts to Suspend Testing and Reinforce Arms-control Initiatives Now Act or the RESTRAIN Act.2.Prohibition on explosive testing of nuclear weapons conducted by the United StatesSection 4210 of the Atomic Energy Defense Act (division D of the Bob Stump National Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2003 (Public Law 107–314; 50 U.S.C. 2530)) is amended—(1)by striking subsection (a) and inserting the following:(a)Prohibition on testingNo explosive testing of a nuclear weapon, or any other nuclear explosion, may be conducted by the United States.; and(2)by adding at the end the following new subsections:(c)Limitation on availability of fundsNone of the funds authorized to be appropriated by an Act authorizing funds for fiscal year 2026 or otherwise made available for any fiscal year may be used—(1)to conduct explosive testing of a nuclear weapon; or(2)for any other nuclear explosion.(d)Rule of construction(1)In generalNothing in this section may be constructed to limit the authority of the United States to conduct a subcritical nuclear test.(2)Subcritical nuclear test definedIn this subsection, the term subcritical nuclear test means a nuclear test of fissile materials that are not capable of sustaining an explosive nuclear chain reaction..
